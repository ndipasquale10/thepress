THE PRESS visual identity
Design Direction · A Bolder Reinvention

The Action Sheet

The Press is a golf betting tracker — score, bet, settle up. This identity leans all the way into the name: not a country-club scorecard, but a tote board and settle-up ledger. A heavy grotesque does the shouting, tabular figures keep the money honest, and a single hot red is spent only where it counts — the press, and what you owe.

Light-first & readableA cool green-grey newsprint that survives direct sun — the round is played outdoors.
The ledger is the heroMoney and scores line up in tabular columns. Green means up, red means owe. No decoration.
Spend red onceVermilion is reserved for the press and the deficit — everywhere else stays quiet.

02 — The Product

Three Screens, Both Editions

The reinvention applied to the app's most-used surfaces — each shown in the daytime Light and Night editions, side by side.

Screen 1

The Games Desk

Setup reads like a listings page. Course, players and game sit in a clean numbered masthead; the eleven betting games become an editorial index — a lettered badge, the name, and a one-line serif dek that finally explains how each one plays.

  • Every game shares one row template, so all eleven look like one family.
  • The chosen game is marked with a red rule and a "PICKED" stamp.
  • Tags — Popular, Team — help newcomers choose fast.

Screen 2

Scoring & the Money Line

The busiest screen, unstacked. A tote-board hole number tops the page; scores are entered as big tabular figures; and a single Money Line ledger keeps the running settle-up in view — the one thing everyone actually wants to see.

  • Boxed cards give way to one calm column with ledger rules.
  • The Press button is the loud, deliberate action — one tap doubles the bet.
  • Card and standings tuck into sheets instead of stacking.

Screen 3

Back Issues & Standings

History becomes an archive of back issues — each finished round a dated entry with the headline result and the settle-up beneath it. The season rolls up into a proper league table, leader flagged in gold.

  • Every round reads as a one-line story, not a data dump.
  • Season standings use tabular net figures — green up, red down.
  • The leader carries the flag; the table is sorted by money, not name.
